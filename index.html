AI Task Taxonomy Evaluation Framework

This framework helps Higher Education staff evaluate tasks for potential AI integration across six dimensions: Nature, Risk, Ethics, Value, Human Element, and Feasibility.

Step 2 of 9: Task Nature & Complexity

How routine and predictable is the task?

Simple rule-following, analysis, or complex creative judgment?

Does the task rely heavily on structured data processing?

Are the task rules and outcomes very clear or highly ambiguous?

Step 3 of 9: Risk, Safety & Security

Potential harm if sensitive data is leaked or misused.

Chance of AI introducing or amplifying unfair bias.

Potential for AI errors causing harm, stress, or negative outcomes.

Impact if AI fails, misbehaves, or causes institutional damage.

Step 4 of 9: Ethical Considerations

Does AI raise concerns about equitable treatment or outcomes?

Is it difficult to explain how AI reaches outputs?

Is it unclear who is accountable for AI errors or outcomes?

Does AI risk undermining human agency, dignity, or autonomy?

Step 5 of 9: Stakeholder Value & Impact

How much could AI enhance learning, access, or support for students?

Could AI reduce tedious workload or enhance staff capabilities?

Can AI improve operations, decision-making, or strategic goals?

Does this use of AI align with institutional values and goals?

Step 6 of 9: Human Element Requirement

Is understanding emotions and interpersonal nuance crucial for the task?

Is coaching, mentorship, or individual support essential for this task?

Does the task require interpreting subtle cues and complex situations?

Does the task involve sensitive communication requiring human discretion?

Step 7 of 9: Feasibility & Implementation

Is the AI technology stable, proven, and reliable for this context?

Is sufficient, high-quality, unbiased data available for AI use?

Are the financial and human resources reasonable compared to expected benefits?

Is integration into existing systems and compliance with institutional policies easy?
